Maven is an interactive virtual community for LGBTQA youth to network, organize and educate for social change. Through social gaming, Maven provides Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer and Ally youth with training, resources and leadership opportunities.  By utilizing a platform that enhances youth engagement, youth will get connected to other LGBTQA youth and community based organizations who serve queer youth.

Gamification Strategies Include:
  • LGBTQA Digital Badging - “Leveling Up” of queer youth activist through achievement points
  • MYearBook phone app - Documenting and archiving the queer youth movement
  • Audre Lorde Library - Data-basing LGBTQA resources
  • Direct Chat - Connecting youth through written dialogue
  • Qstreet Neighborhood - Building of only community based organizations serving queer youth and GSAs/LGBTQA clubs hosted in the cyber world.
Maven will:
  • Provide a safe space online for LGBTQA youth from rural to urban communities to network, learn and organize with peers locally and globally.
  • Provide LGBTQA youth training and tools to utilize in their local communities and virtually.
  • Connect youth serving organizations to provide a global community of resources for LGBTQA youth.
  • Capture a history of the LGBTQA youth movement worldwide.
Maven aims to impact:
  • Isolation of LGBTQ youth, especially in rural and low-income areas.
  • Lack of access to LGBTQ resources and fragmented youth serving organizations.
  • Limited LGBTQ youth in leadership positions or opportunities made available to LGBTQ youth.
  • Cyber bullying experienced on social networking sites due to homophobia and transphobia.
